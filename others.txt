Why we take Top 20 Node Types and Predicated Explaination

Semantic web graphs heavily follow a Power-Law (Zipfian) distribution. 
A tiny fraction of relations (like publication, author, project) appear thousands of times, 
while hundreds of highly specific predicates or literal attributes appear only once or twice across the entire network. 
Including these ultra-rare relations introduces extreme sparsity into the R-GCN's relation-specific adjacency matrices, 
leading to zero-gradient updates for those channels during backpropagation.
Filtering for the top 20 preserves the dense, highly-connected core of the network.
